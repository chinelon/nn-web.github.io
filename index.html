Dr. Nneka Nwobbi's Mission to Transform Childhood Cancer Care in Nigeria

Dr. Nneka Nwobbi’s journey is one of dedication, compassion, and innovation. Her career began in medicine, but her impact extends far beyond the walls of the hospital. As a medical doctor, she witnessed firsthand the struggles faced by children with cancer and their families in Nigeria. This led her to establish the Children Living With Cancer Foundation in 2003, marking the inception of Nigeria’s first nonprofit charity focused on childhood cancer.
The foundation’s mission is multifaceted: it not only provides financial support for medical treatments, surgeries, and investigations but also offers counseling services to children and their families. Through relentless advocacy and awareness campaigns, Dr. Nwobbi has brought significant attention to the plight of children battling cancer in Nigeria. Her efforts have resulted in over 3,000 children receiving critical support, making a substantial difference in their lives and health outcomes.​

Despite her demanding medical career, Dr. Nwobbi's entrepreneurial spirit led her to found Chilo’s Foods in 2018. This venture arose from her need to ensure a steady supply of quality food amidst Nigeria's erratic electricity supply, prompting her to dry food in bulk. The popularity of this practical solution among her friends and family encouraged her to expand the service.
Dr. Nwobbi's work has transformed many lives and set a precedent for charitable healthcare initiatives in Nigeria. Her entrepreneurial venture, Chilo’s Foods, underscores her commitment to improving her community's quality of life. Through her multifaceted career, Dr. Nwobbi continues to inspire and make a profound societal impact.
